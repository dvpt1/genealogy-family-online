Introduction

Genealogical information is, as a rule, legally formalized and documented in appropriate standard documents that establish kinship, family tree ties, and family history. Establishing family tree ties requires information on at least two generations of the family.

The process of collecting and analyzing genealogical information is painstaking and requires diligence. It involves searching for and studying archival records, parish registers, confession records, census reports, and other historical documents containing information about the birth, marriage, death, place of residence, and social status of ancestors.

In addition to official documents, oral traditions, family legends, and photographs can be valuable sources of genealogical information. They allow us to reconstruct the lives of our ancestors and learn about their occupations, traditions, and customs. It is important to remember that oral traditions may contain inaccuracies and distortions, so they should be compared with documentary data.

The result of genealogical research is the creation of a family tree, which clearly displays the connections between generations of a family. The family tree can be presented as a diagram, table, or other graphical representation. It allows you to see the history of your family over time, trace the migrations of your ancestors, and identify recurring names and surnames.

Genealogical information has not only historical but also practical value. It can be used to confirm kinship when applying for an inheritance, establishing citizenship, or receiving social benefits. Furthermore, knowing your family tree helps you understand your identity, feel a connection to the past, and understand your place in the world.

Family Member List

Launch your website with the platform installed.

After launching the website, the main program window - Person List - appears.

Creating a New and Updating a Card

A special form is used to enter data. Fill in the form fields.

To enter information about a family member, click on the button with the person icon on a sheet of paper in the menu bar. When asked, the program asks "Adding a row. Add?", click the "OK" button. A new row will be added to the end of the list (when you first run the program, there is one blank row in the list).

Place the cursor with the mouse or the control keys on a cell in the "SURNAME, FIRST NAME, PATRONYMIC" column and press Enter or double-click the mouse. Enter the full name. Press Enter or click in another field.

Place the cursor on a cell in the "ARRIVER" column and press Enter or double-click. The date is entered in the "Day. Month. Year" format. Enter the date of birth using the numeric keypad or select it from the calendar . Press Enter. To delete the date, press the Test key.ate (Space).

If the person is no longer alive, place the cursor in the "GONE" column and press Enter or double-click. Enter the birth date using the numeric keypad or select it from the calendar. Press Enter. To delete a date, press Space.

Place the cursor in the "GENDER" column and press Enter or double-click. Select gender from the drop-down list. Press Enter or click another field.

For further research and restoration of family tree, it is very important to know the exact place of birth.

Place the cursor in the "PLACE OF BIRTH" column and press Enter or double-click. Enter the person's exact known or estimated place of birth.

Place your cursor in the "PLACE OF DEATH" column and press Enter or double-click. Enter the person's exact known or estimated place of death.

Place your cursor in the "Biography Panel" window and complete the biography of your family member.

The biography is free-form. You can include biographical information: where they studied, where they worked, their occupation, etc., as well as memorable dates, anniversaries, and milestones. In short, everything you know about him or what you want to keep.

Mother/Father

Place the cursor in the "FATHER" column and press Enter or double-click. You will be presented with a list of all male family members in the list. Therefore, please note that the "GENDER" field is required. Select the father from the drop-down list. All family members in the list are listed alphabetically. To quickly find a specific family member in the list, start typing their last name and the program will help you find the person you need. Press Enter or click another field.

Place the cursor in the "MOTHER" column and press Enter or double-click. You will be presented with a list of all female family members in the list. Therefore, please note that the "GENDER" field is required. Select the mother from the drop-down list. All family members in the list are listed alphabetically. To quickly find a family member in the list, start typing their last name, and the program will help you find them. Press Enter or click on another field.

Spouses

To enter the full name of the wife or husband, place the cursor in the cell in the "HUSBAND-WIFE" column and press Enter or double-click. Select the wife or husband from the list.

If you need to add multiple people to this list, you can do so using the input form.

Place of Residence

To enter the Place of Residence, place the cursor on the Plus button and click. Select and enter your Place of Life in the pop-up window.

Enter your dates of life "from" - "to".

Other fields

Enter other fields: "Occupation", "Nationality", "Education", "Religion".

The "Note" field is free-form.
